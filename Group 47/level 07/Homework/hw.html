Georgian tradition

The Baptism

Georgia is one of the oldest Christian countries, and religious practices have shaped the culture for centuries. Easter and Christmas are celebrated with special services, meals, and traditional songs. The Georgian Orthodox Church plays a central role in the daily lives of the people, and many traditions revolve around church holidays, feasts, and rituals.

WEDDING

Georgian weddings are deeply traditional and are a celebration of family and community. They often include traditional dances such as the Kartuli or Lezginka and music played with instruments like the panduri and doli. The rituals also include the "bride price" and "kitchen blessing," where the bride is presented with gifts and food as part of the ceremony.
