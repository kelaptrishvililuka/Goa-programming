300 is a 2006 American historical epic war film based on the graphic novel by Frank Miller. It was directed by Zack Snyder, and the main roles were played by Gerard Butler (King Leonidas) and Lena Headey. Movie Overview: The film depicts the Battle of Thermopylae, where King Leonidas of Sparta leads a small army of 300 soldiers to defend against the massive Persian Empire led by King Xerxes. It portrays the bravery of the Spartans and their determination to resist overwhelming odds, symbolizing courage and heroism. The film emphasizes the visual spectacle of battle and dramatic moments. Popularity: The movie was released in 2006 and became an immediate hit, praised for its striking visual style, which used heavy stylization and slow-motion effects. Its cinematic style and portrayal of historical heroism made it highly influential and it gained a strong fan following.
